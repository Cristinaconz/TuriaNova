Metropolitan Analysis · 04
Shift-Share — True Competitiveness
Employment change decomposition 2008–2025 · Positive CE = local outperformance vs national average
COMPETITIVE EFFECT (CE) · thousands of jobs · Valencia vs Spain
NS = national share · IM = industry mix · CE = local competitive residual
✅ Industry · CE +17.96K
Valencia outperforms its national peers. Industryl clusters are genuinely competitive. Confirms rising LQ. The strongest positive signal in the full analysis.
⚠️ Services · CE −26,92K
The dominant engine grows slower than the national average. Valencia is a follower, not a leader. If high-value services shift to Madrid/Barcelona, absorption could slow.
❌ Construction · CE −19,67K
Contraction more severe than the national average. Limited capacity → upward cost pressure, but eliminates oversupply risk.

Synthesis: model anchored in a competitive industrial base, dependent on national services momentum. Solid but without a differentiation premium.

Conclusion · Investment Thesis
BTR mid-market Valencia:
cuatro evidencias, una recomendación
01 · Metropolitan
Volume-based + affordability-constrained model → real demand concentrated in mid-market rental. Industryl base (CE+) guarantees sustained mid-income.
02 · Macro
+46,786 units deficit (2.69:1). Robust. Completions are the bottleneck. No solution within the project horizon.
03 · Micro CDT
TAI 4,71 · CPI 1,281 · D/S 9,8:1. Base-case absorption: ~10 months. La Fe as primary anchor.
04 · Risk
Not demand. It's construction cost escalation. Budget contingency +10–15%. Fixed-price contracts where feasible.
FINAL RECOMMENDATION
Desarrollo BTR de 60 unidades (50% 2-hab / 50% 3-hab) a €1.750–1.900/mes,
entrega Q4 2026, estabilización proyectada en ~10 meses.
The macro deficit makes demand shortfall remote. The real risk is in execution.
